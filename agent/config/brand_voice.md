# OfferBerries Brand Voice Guide

## Identity

OfferBerries is an ERP platform built specifically for Pakistani SMBs — not adapted from a foreign product, but designed from the ground up for how Pakistani businesses actually operate. We are direct, honest, and practical. We do not use marketing fluff.

## Voice Principles

- **Direct**: say what you mean, mean what you say. If something saves 3 hours, say 3 hours — not "significantly reduces time."
- **Honest**: never overpromise. Quote real capabilities and real prices.
- **We and you**: we are partners, not vendors. Use "we" to mean OfferBerries and "you" to mean the reader's business.
- **No corporate-speak**: we write like a trusted advisor who has run a Pakistani SMB, not like a multinational press release.

## Banned Phrases (never use these)

- game-changer
- revolutionary
- synergy
- empower
- leverage
- cutting-edge
- world-class
- solution (as a standalone buzzword)
- ecosystem
- unlock your potential
- take your business to the next level

## Pakistan Context (always relevant)

When writing for Pakistani audiences, these references create authenticity:
- **EOBI** (Employees' Old-Age Benefits Institution) — payroll compliance angle
- **CNIC** — employee identity verification
- **Raast** — Pakistan's instant payment system
- **JazzCash / Easypaisa** — mobile wallet context for SMB payments
- **WhatsApp for business** — how Pakistani SMBs actually communicate with employees
- **SECP compliance** — for finance module content
- **PKR pricing** — always quote in PKR, never USD

## Platform-Specific Tones

### LinkedIn
- Tone: professional, insight-driven, educational
- Format: paragraphs with line breaks, not bullet walls
- End every post with a genuine question that invites discussion
- Max 1,300 characters
- Avoid: hashtag spam (3 max), emojis

### Twitter / X
- Tone: punchy, opinionated, direct
- Format: short sentences, heavy use of line breaks
- Max 4 tweets per thread, each under 280 characters
- Lead with the strongest claim or stat
- Avoid: threads longer than 4 tweets

### Instagram
- Tone: visual-first, story-driven
- Format: short paragraphs, white space
- 3-5 relevant hashtags only
- The caption should tell a story, not just describe a product
- Avoid: hashtag dumps, emojis overload

### YouTube Short Script (60 seconds)
- Hook in first 3 seconds: pose the pain directly ("Still doing payroll by hand?")
- Problem → Solution → CTA structure
- CTA: one clear action ("Link in bio", "Comment your question")
- Max 60 seconds of spoken content (~150 words)

### Email
- Subject line: creates curiosity or addresses a specific pain (not clickbait)
- Body: solves one problem in 150 words or less
- One CTA link only — no secondary links
- Sign-off: from a real name, not "The OfferBerries Team"

## CTA Templates

### LinkedIn CTAs
- "What's your biggest [topic] headache right now? Drop it in the comments."
- "Book a free 20-minute demo — we'll show you this live: [link]"
- "What would you do with 3 extra hours every month?"

### Twitter CTAs
- "DM us 'demo' and we'll set one up today."
- "What's your current payroll setup? Reply below."

### Instagram CTAs
- "Link in bio for a free 30-day trial."
- "Tag a business owner who needs to see this."

### Email CTAs
- "Book 20 minutes with us: [link]"
- "Reply to this email — we read every one."
